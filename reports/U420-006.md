# USB Cable Validation Report — Tripp Lite U420-006

**Cable:** Tripp Lite U420-006 — USB-C to USB-C, 6 ft (1.83 m), USB 3.1 Gen 1 (5 Gbps), passive
**Sample:** `u420_006_sample_1`
**Operator:** Ekow Taylor, Network Infrastructure Services
**Date:** May 15, 2026
**Test machine:** MacBook Air M5, macOS 26.3 (Darwin 25.x), Python 3.14, BrainStem SDK
**Hub:** Acroname USBHub3c — model code 24, serial `0x68D1A7BE`, 4 downstream ports
**Signal chain:** MacBook M5 → U420-006 (cable under test) → USBHub3c (no Ramsey STE4453M4 enclosure)
**Raw data:** [`reports/U420-006/raw/`](./U420-006/raw/) (per-test CSV snapshots, preserved outside the gitignored `results/`)
**Comparison baseline:** [`reports/U420-010.md`](./U420-010.md) (10 ft / 3.05 m, same hub & methodology)

---

## Verdict

**QUALIFIED for its rated 5 Gbps (USB 3.1 Gen 1) use.**
142/142 cycles across both hub interfaces re-negotiated at ≥ rated speed, with zero
enumeration timeouts and zero USB-2 fallbacks. Behaviour is statistically
indistinguishable from the U420-010 — **cable length (1.83 m vs 3.05 m) had no
meaningful effect** at rated speed or on reliability.

## Finding — 20 Gbps link is not 100 % reproducible across reconnects (same as U420-010)

Per-cycle speed logging shows the data-port link did not always re-train at full
20 Gbps after a port cycle — identical in character to the U420-010:

| Data-port cycling | 20 Gbps | 5 Gbps re-train |
|---|---|---|
| 5.1.2 ×20 | 17 | 3 |
| 5.2.3 ×50 | 47 | 3 |
| **Combined ×70** | **64 (91.4 %)** | **6 (8.6 %)** |

Every one of these 6 cycles is still **PASS** — 5 Gbps is exactly the U420-006's
rated speed, so all 70 reconnects met spec. As with the U420-010, the cable's
*over-spec* 20 Gbps training (4× its 5 Gbps rating) is not perfectly reproducible
across hot reconnects. No USB-2 fallbacks, no enumeration timeouts.

## Head-to-head vs U420-010

| Metric | U420-006 (1.83 m) | U420-010 (3.05 m) |
|---|---|---|
| Control port — all tests | 71/71 PASS, all 5 Gbps | 71/71 PASS, all 5 Gbps |
| Data port — all tests | 71/71 PASS | 71/71 PASS |
| 5.1.1 data initial link | 20 Gbps | 20 Gbps |
| Data reconnect re-train to 5 Gbps | 6 / 70 (8.6 %) | 4 / 70 (5.7 %) |
| USB-2 fallbacks / enum timeouts / FAIL | 0 / 0 / 0 | 0 / 0 / 0 |

The 8.6 % vs 5.7 % difference is **2 events out of 70** — within statistical noise
for this sample size; the cables are effectively equivalent. Notably the *shorter*
cable did not show *better* 20 Gbps reproducibility, indicating the occasional
re-train to 5 Gbps is dominated by host/hub link-training behaviour at 20 Gbps
(4× spec), not by cable length or signal attenuation. **The U420-010's extra
1.2 m introduces no measurable penalty at the rated 5 Gbps.**

## Recommendation

| If the deployment… | Then… |
|---|---|
| Uses the U420-006 at its **rated 5 Gbps** | **Approved.** 70/70 cycles ≥ rated speed, no timeouts, no USB-2 fallback. |

| Intends to **rely on the 20 Gbps over-spec link** | Accept a ≈ 6–9 % re-train-to-5 Gbps rate after reconnects for *either* cable, **or** pin the hub data port to 5 Gbps for deterministic behaviour. |
