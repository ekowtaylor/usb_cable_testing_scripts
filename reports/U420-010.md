**Sample:** `u420_010_sample_1`
**Operator:** Ekow Taylor, Network Infrastructure Services
**Date:** May 15, 2026
**Test machine:** MacBook Air M5, macOS 26.3 (Darwin 25.x), Python 3.14, BrainStem SDK
**Hub:** Acroname USBHub3c — model code 24, serial `0x68D1A7BE`, 4 downstream ports
**Signal chain:** MacBook M5 → U420-010 (cable under test) → USBHub3c (no Ramsey STE4453M4 enclosure)
**Raw data:** [`reports/U420-010/raw/`](./U420-010/raw/) (per-test CSV snapshots, preserved outside the gitignored `results/`)

## Verdict

**QUALIFIED for its rated 5 Gbps (USB 3.1 Gen 1) use.**
70/70 hot-reconnect cycles re-negotiated at ≥ rated speed, with zero enumeration timeouts and zero USB-2 fallbacks across both hub interfaces. One caveat applies if the deployment intends to rely on the cable's *over-spec* 20 Gbps link — see [Finding 2](#finding-2--20-gbps-link-is-not-100-reproducible-across-reconnects).

## Finding 1 — Original enumeration-timeout failures were re-enumeration latency, not the cable

A prior session (30 s polling window) recorded data-port failures: **5.1.2 = 19/20** and **5.2.3 = 47/50**, four cases total — all *enumeration timeouts* (device not found within 30 s), never speed fallbacks, every one self-recovering on the next cycle.

This run used a **60 s polling window** (the prior session's own recommended next step). With the identical hub, cable, and topology, the data port now passes **70/70** with **zero timeouts**.

**Conclusion:** the earlier ~5 % failure rate was macOS USB re-enumeration latency for the 20 Gbps SuperSpeed+ link occasionally exceeding 30 s — **not** a U420-010 signal-integrity defect. The 60 s window fully absorbs it.

---

## Finding 2 — 20 Gbps link is not 100 % reproducible across reconnects

Per-cycle speed logging (added this session) shows the data-port link did not always re-train at full 20 Gbps after a port cycle:

| Test (data port) | 20 Gbps (SuperSpeed+ 2x1) | 5 Gbps (SuperSpeed) |
|---|---|---|
| 5.1.2 ×20 | 19 | 1 |
| 5.2.3 ×50 | 47 | 3 |
| **Combined ×70** | **66 (94.3 %)** | **4 (5.7 %)** |

These 4 cycles are still **PASS** — 5 Gbps is exactly the U420-010's rated speed, so every cycle met spec. But the cable's *over-spec* 20 Gbps training (4× its 5 Gbps rating, at 3 m passive — near the edge of passive signaling margin) is **not perfectly reproducible across hot reconnects**, dropping to its rated 5 Gbps on ≈ 5.7 % of cycles.

> The original test suite (pass = USBSpeed ≥ 3, no per-cycle speed capture) would have reported a clean pass and **hidden this entirely**. It is visible only because results now log the negotiated `speed_label` for every cycle.

Control-port cycles were uniformly 5 Gbps — expected; the USBHub3c Stem port is hardware-capped at 5 Gbps regardless of cable, so control-port runs validate the harness, not cable signal margin.

## Recommendation

| If the deployment… | Then… |
|---|---|
| Uses the U420-010 at its **rated 5 Gbps** | **Approved.** 70/70 cycles ≥ rated speed, no timeouts, no USB-2 fallback. |
| Intends to **rely on the 20 Gbps over-spec link** | Accept the ≈ 6 % re-train-to-5 Gbps rate after reconnects, **or** pin the hub data port to 5 Gbps (`setSuperSpeedDataDisable` / `PORT_MODE_SUPER_SPEED_1_ENABLE`) for deterministic behavior. |
| Targets the **rack environment** | Re-test with the Ramsey STE4453M4 enclosure in-chain (tests 5.1.3 / 5.2.x / 5.3.x) before volume procurement. |
